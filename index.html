Research Publications

My contributions to the fields of computer vision and geospatial AI

Have Foundational Models Seen Satellite Images?

This research investigates the capabilities and limitations of foundation models like CLIP, DALL-E, and GPT-4V in understanding and processing satellite imagery data. We propose novel benchmarks to evaluate these models and identify key areas for improvement in geospatial understanding. Our findings reveal significant gaps in how foundation models interpret spectral information and geospatial context compared to specialized remote sensing models.

IGARSS, 2023
View Paper Presentation Code

GeoEngine: A Platform for Production-Ready Geospatial Research

We present GeoEngine, an end-to-end platform for managing satellite imagery processing that bridges the gap between research and production in geospatial AI. The platform integrates dataset curation, model training, inference orchestration, and deployment tools specifically designed for geospatial workflows. Our implementation enables efficient development and deployment of over 30 AI solutions for applications ranging from precision agriculture to disaster response, with a 94% reduction in model deployment time compared to traditional approaches.

CVPR, 2022
View Paper Presentation Code

QFabric: Multi-Task Change Detection Dataset

We introduce QFabric, a comprehensive dataset for multi-task change detection in satellite imagery. The dataset contains over 50,000 image pairs with pixel-level annotations across five change detection tasks: land-use change, building development, deforestation, flooding, and agricultural shifts. We benchmark various deep learning architectures and propose a novel multi-task learning framework that leverages shared representations across tasks. Our results demonstrate significant improvements in detection accuracy and generalization to new geographic regions compared to single-task approaches.

CVPR, 2021
